<p><p>Non-woven fabrics are classified into three types based on their manufacturing process. Dry-laid type non-woven fabrics are made by entangling fibers through a dry process, such as needle punching. Spunmelt type non-woven fabrics are produced by melting and extruding synthetic fibers and then bonding them together to form a fabric. Wet-laid type non-woven fabrics are created by mixing fiber suspensions in a liquid, which is then formed into a sheet and dried. Each type has its own unique characteristics and applications in various industries.</p></p>
