<p><p>The microencapsulated food ingredients market can be classified into several types based on the technologies used. Spray technologies involve spraying a coating material onto the food ingredient to form a protective shell. Emulsion technologies involve mixing the food ingredient with an emulsion to form tiny droplets that are then hardened. Dripping technologies involve dripping the food ingredient into a hardening solution to create a solid shell. Other technologies may include methods such as coacervation and extrusion. These different types of technologies enable the production of microencapsulated food ingredients with various properties and applications.</p></p>
