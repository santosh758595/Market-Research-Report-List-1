<p><p>The animal anti-rabies vaccine market includes applications for both pre-exposure prophylaxis and post-exposure prophylaxis. Pre-exposure prophylaxis refers to vaccinating animals before any potential rabies exposure to prevent them from contracting the disease. This is especially important for high-risk animals such as those living in rabies-endemic areas or those likely to come into contact with infected animals. On the other hand, post-exposure prophylaxis involves vaccination after potential rabies exposure to prevent the development of the disease. This is typically recommended for animals that have been bitten by rabid animals to minimize the risk of infection.</p></p>
