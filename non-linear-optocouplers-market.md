<p><p>Non-linear optocouplers are electronic devices used for isolating and transmitting signals between two electrical circuits without any direct electrical connection. They are available in various types including photovoltaic-output, transistor-output, triac-output, thyristor-output, IC-output, and others. Photovoltaic-output optocouplers use a light-sensitive photovoltaic diode to convert the incoming light into an electrical signal. Transistor-output optocouplers utilize a transistor to amplify the incoming signal. Triac-output and thyristor-output optocouplers are designed to control AC power by switching on and off. IC-output optocouplers integrate multiple components into a single chip, while others include unique features or combinations of different outputs.</p></p>
