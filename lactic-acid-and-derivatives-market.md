<p><p>Lactic acid is a colorless or yellowish organic acid that is formed in the muscles during strenuous exercise when oxygen levels are low. It is commonly used as a flavoring agent, pH regulator, and antimicrobial agent in food and beverage products. In the pharmaceutical industry, lactic acid is used in the production of various drugs and as a pH regulator in topical formulations. In personal care products, it is used as a moisturizer, exfoliant, and pH adjuster. Lactic acid derivatives, such as lactides and polylactic acid (PLA), are used in the production of bioplastics and biodegradable packaging materials.</p><p>The global lactic acid and derivatives market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
