<p><p>The medical grade hydroxypropyl cellulose (HPC) market can be categorized into two types: L-HPC and H-HPC. L-HPC stands for low-substituted hydroxypropyl cellulose, while H-HPC refers to high-substituted hydroxypropyl cellulose. Both types are widely used in the healthcare industry for their unique properties. L-HPC has a lower degree of substitution and is predominantly used as a binder and thickening agent in pharmaceutical formulations. On the other hand, H-HPC has a higher degree of substitution and is primarily employed as a controlled-release agent in drug delivery systems.</p></p>
