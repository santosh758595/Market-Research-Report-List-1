<p><p>Butanes, also known as butane isomers, are hydrocarbon gases that exist in four different forms: n-butane, isobutane, neo-butane, and iso-neo-butane. They are colorless and odorless gases that can be liquefied under moderate pressure, making them suitable for use in various industries.</p><p>The global butanes market is expected to experience significant growth over the forecast period. The market is primarily driven by the increasing demand for liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) as a cooking fuel in residential and commercial sectors. The growth of the automotive industry, particularly in emerging economies, is also contributing to the demand for butanes as fuel for vehicles. Additionally, the growing petrochemical industry is creating a substantial demand for butanes as feedstock for the production of chemicals such as ethylene and propylene.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing adoption of natural gas liquids (NGLs) by the energy industry for fuel blending is further fueling the growth of the butanes market. Butanes, being a vital component of NGLs, are experiencing a surge in demand. However, stringent government regulations regarding the usage of hydrocarbon gases due to their impact on the environment may pose a challenge to market growth.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, Asia Pacific is expected to dominate the butanes market during the forecast period. North America and Europe are also significant markets, primarily driven by the growing petrochemical industry and the increasing use of NGLs.</p><p>Overall, the butanes market is projected to witness a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5% during the forecast period.
