<p><p>Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat joint problems. Arthroscopic instruments are specialized tools used during this procedure to visualize, diagnose, and treat various joint conditions. These instruments consist of a small camera, called an arthroscope, along with other instruments such as probes, graspers, shavers, and drills, which are inserted into the joint through small incisions.</p><p>The arthroscopic instruments market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of joint disorders, such as osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis, is a key factor driving market growth. Additionally, advancements in arthroscopic techniques and instruments, such as the introduction of disposable arthroscopic instruments and improved imaging systems, are further fueling market expansion.</p><p>Moreover, the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures, shorter hospital stays, and faster recovery times is boosting the adoption of arthroscopic instruments. The shift from traditional open surgeries to arthroscopic procedures is also contributing to market growth.</p><p>Geographically, North America dominates the arthroscopic instruments market due to the high prevalence of joint disorders and favorable reimbursement policies. Technological advancements, increasing prevalence of joint disorders, and a shift towards minimally invasive procedures are key factors contributing to market growth.
