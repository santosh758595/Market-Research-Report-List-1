<p><p>High-purity iodine is classified into three main market types: saltpeter mineral iodine, underground brine iodine, and seaweed iodine. Saltpeter mineral iodine is derived from natural deposits of iodine-containing mineral ores, such as caliche ore. Underground brine iodine is obtained through the extraction of iodine-rich brine solutions found deep underground. Seaweed iodine comes from the purification of iodine obtained from seaweed, primarily from the Laminaria genus. These market types supply high-quality iodine necessary for various industries, including pharmaceuticals, food additives, and electronics.</p></p>
