<p><p>Aneurysm coiling and embolization devices are medical devices used in the treatment of brain aneurysms, which are weakened blood vessels that bulge and may rupture. These devices are inserted into the blood vessels through a minimally invasive procedure to block off the aneurysm and prevent blood flow to it, reducing the risk of rupture.</p><p>The global aneurysm coiling & embolization devices market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 9.6% during the forecast period. The market is driven by factors such as increasing incidence of brain aneurysms, advancements in coiling and embolization techniques, and rising preference for minimally invasive procedures. Manufacturers are focusing on improving the efficacy and safety of these devices to enhance patient outcomes. For instance, detachable coils with advanced designs and coatings are being introduced to improve the packing density and stability within the aneurysm. Furthermore, the use of liquid embolic agents, such as flow diverters and flow disrupters, is gaining popularity due to their ability to redirect blood flow away from the aneurysm.</p><p>Geographically, North America dominates the aneurysm coiling & embolization devices market, owing to the high prevalence of brain aneurysms and well-established healthcare infrastructure. <p><ul><li>Embolic Coils</li><li>Flow Diversion Devices</li><li>Liquid Embolic Agents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Aneurysm coiling and embolization devices are used for the treatment of brain aneurysms. Embolic coils are small metallic coils inserted into an aneurysm to promote blood clotting and prevent rupture. Flow diversion devices are stent-like implants that redirect blood flow away from the aneurysm. Liquid embolic agents are injectable substances that solidify inside the aneurysm, cutting off blood flow and preventing rupture. These devices are utilized in the treatment of different types of aneurysms, providing options for physicians to select the most suitable treatment approach.</p></p>
