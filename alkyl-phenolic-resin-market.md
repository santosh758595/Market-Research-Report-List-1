Lump resin is solid and irregularly shaped, often used in applications that require higher melting points and greater strength. On the other hand, flake resin is in the form of small, flat pieces and is preferred for its ease of handling and uniformity. Both types offer excellent adhesion and chemical resistance, making them suitable for adhesives, coatings, and molding compounds in industries such as automotive, construction, and electronics.</p></p>
