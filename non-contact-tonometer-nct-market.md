<p><p>A non-contact tonometer (NCT) is a medical device used to measure the intraocular pressure (IOP) of the eye without touching it. It uses a puff of air to flatten the cornea and calculates the IOP based on the changes in the corneal surface. NCTs are commonly used in the diagnosis and management of glaucoma, a condition that causes damage to the optic nerve.</p><p>The non-contact tonometer market is expected to experience steady growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of glaucoma, coupled with the growing aging population, is driving the demand for NCTs. As the global population continues to age, the incidence of eye disorders, including glaucoma, is expected to rise, fueling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in NCTs are likely to contribute to market expansion. The development of portable devices with improved accuracy and ease of use is attracting healthcare professionals and driving adoption. Additionally, the integration of NCTs with electronic health record systems and telemedicine is streamlining the diagnosis and management of eye conditions.</p><p>The COVID-19 pandemic has also accelerated the adoption of non-contact tonometers. The need for contactless clinical measurements to minimize the risk of viral transmission has increased the demand for NCTs. This trend is expected to continue in the future, boosting market growth.</p><p>Overall, the non-contact tonometer market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 9.2% during the forecasted period. Factors such as the rising prevalence of glaucoma, technological advancements, and the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic are expected to drive the market's future outlook.</p></p>
