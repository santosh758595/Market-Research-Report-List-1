<p><p>The high frequency inductors market consists of three main types: wire wound, film, and multilayer. Wire wound inductors are made by winding copper wire around a core material. Film inductors are created by depositing a thin film of conductive material on a non-conductive substrate. Multilayer inductors involve stacking several thin film layers on top of each other. These types of inductors are used in high frequency applications, such as communication devices, because they have low resistance and high efficiency in transmitting and storing electrical energy.</p></p>
