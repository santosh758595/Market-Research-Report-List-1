<p><p>SDHI fungicides, such as Carboxin, Oxycarboxin, Mepronil, Flutolanil, Benodanil, and Fenfuram, are widely used in the agricultural industry to control fungal diseases in crops. Carboxin and Oxycarboxin inhibit the germination of fungal spores, while Mepronil disrupts the growth of fungal mycelium. Flutolanil targets the energy production process of fungi, Benodanil acts on their cell membranes, and Fenfuram interferes with their basic metabolic functions. These different types of SDHI fungicides provide farmers with effective options for protecting their crops against various fungal pathogens.</p></p>
