<p><p>Battery separators for hybrid and electric vehicles can be classified into two main types - Type I and Type II. Type I separators are usually made from polyolefin materials such as polyethylene and polypropylene. They have a relatively lower cost and provide good thermal stability and electrolyte wettability. On the other hand, Type II separators are manufactured using advanced materials like ceramic or nanofiber composites. They offer improved thermal resistance, enhanced safety, and higher performance characteristics. Type II separators tend to be more expensive but are preferred for high-performance applications.</p></p>
