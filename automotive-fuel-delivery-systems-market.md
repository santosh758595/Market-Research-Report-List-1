<p><p>Automotive Fuel Delivery Systems refers to the mechanisms used to deliver fuel to the engine in vehicles. Pump-Line-Nozzle Delivery Systems involve a mechanical pump which pressurizes the fuel and delivers it directly to each engine cylinder through individual lines and nozzles. Common Rail Delivery Systems use a high-pressure fuel rail to store and distribute fuel to multiple injectors simultaneously, allowing for more precise and efficient fuel delivery. Other fuel delivery systems include electronic fuel injection systems, which use sensors and electronic controls to regulate fuel flow and optimize engine performance.</p></p>
