<p><p>The motor lubricating oil market can be divided into two types: Type I and Type II. Type I oils are conventional mineral-based oils commonly used in older vehicles and machinery. They provide basic lubrication and protection against wear and corrosion. Type II oils, on the other hand, are advanced synthetic or semi-synthetic oils that offer superior performance and increased engine longevity. These oils are often utilized in newer engines and high-performance vehicles, as they have enhanced thermal stability, viscosity control, and oxidation resistance.</p></p>
