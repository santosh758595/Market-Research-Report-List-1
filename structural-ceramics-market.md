<p><p>Structural ceramics refer to a class of ceramic materials used in various industrial applications due to their exceptional strength, hardness, and temperature resistance. There are two main types of structural ceramics: Oxide Ceramics and Non-Oxide Ceramics. </p><p>Oxide Ceramics are made of combinations of metallic and non-metallic elements, such as alumina or zirconia, and are known for their high thermal stability and electrical insulation properties. They are commonly used in applications like cutting tools, insulators, and protective coatings.</p><p>On the other hand, Non-Oxide Ceramics are composed of predominantly non-metallic elements, such as silicon nitride or silicon carbide. They possess superior mechanical properties, including high strength and toughness, making them suitable for applications like jet engines, automotive parts, and armor systems.</p><p>Both types of structural ceramics play a crucial role in various industries, offering exceptional performance in demanding environments where other materials may fail.</p></p>
