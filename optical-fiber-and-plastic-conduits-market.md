<p><p>Optical fiber refers to a thin, flexible, and transparent fiber made of glass or plastic that is used for transmitting information in the form of light signals. It offers several advantages over traditional copper wires, such as higher bandwidth, faster data transfer rates, longer transmission distances, and immunity to electromagnetic interference. Optical fiber is widely used in telecommunication networks, internet connections, cable television, and various industrial applications.</p><p>Plastic conduits, on the other hand, are protective tunnels or channels made from plastic materials such as PVC or polyethylene. They are used for enclosing and protecting optical fiber cables, providing insulation, and facilitating easy installation and maintenance. Plastic conduits offer flexibility, durability, and resistance to environmental factors like moisture, heat, and corrosive chemicals.</p><p>The optical fiber and plastic conduits market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing demand for high-speed internet connections, rapid digitalization, and the rising adoption of cloud-based services.
