<p><p>Pharmaceutical hot melt extrusion refers to a process used in the pharmaceutical industry to produce solid dosage forms. The market is categorized into two types: twin-screw extruders and single-screw extruders. Twin-screw extruders utilize two intermeshing screws to melt and mix materials, offering advantages like better blending and homogeneity. Single-screw extruders, on the other hand, use one screw to melt and convey materials but are simpler and cost-effective. Both types play a crucial role in the pharmaceutical industry by enabling efficient and controlled drug formulation processes.</p></p>
