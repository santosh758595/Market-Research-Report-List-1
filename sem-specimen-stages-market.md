<p><p>SEM (Scanning Electron Microscope) specimen stages refer to the platforms where samples are placed and analyzed under the microscope. The heating stage is a type of specimen stage that enables researchers to heat the sample during analysis, allowing for the observation of changes in its structure and behavior at elevated temperatures. Conversely, the cooling stage is a specimen stage that facilitates the cooling of the sample, enabling researchers to study its characteristics at low temperatures. Both heating and cooling stages are essential tools in SEM analysis for investigating the thermal properties and behavior of materials.</p></p>
