<p><p>Ferrochrome alloy, also known as ferrochromium, is an alloy of iron and chromium. It is predominantly used in the production of stainless steel, as it imparts corrosion resistance, heat resistance, and other desirable properties to the steel. Ferrochrome alloy is produced by smelting chromite ore in electric arc furnaces, along with other raw materials such as coke, quartz, and lime.</p><p>The global ferrochrome alloy market is expected to witness healthy growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for stainless steel in various industries such as construction, automotive, and aerospace is the key driver for the market.
